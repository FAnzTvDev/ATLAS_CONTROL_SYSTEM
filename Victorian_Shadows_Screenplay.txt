VICTORIAN SHADOWS

"The Estate Sale"

Written for ATLAS Production System
Series Pilot — 10-Minute Short Film



FADE IN:

INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - GRAND FOYER - MORNING

A once-magnificent Victorian foyer now draped in dust sheets. Morning light filters through stained glass windows, casting fractured colors across marble floors. A crystal chandelier hangs dark overhead.

ELEANOR VOSS (34, sharp features, auburn hair pulled back severely, wearing a tailored charcoal blazer over a black turtleneck) steps through the front doors, briefcase in hand. She surveys the space with cold professional detachment.

Behind her, THOMAS BLACKWOOD (62, distinguished silver hair, weathered face lined with grief, wearing a rumpled navy suit that was once expensive) enters slowly, one hand trailing along the banister.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
She would have hated this. Strangers
pawing through her things.

ELEANOR VOSS
Mr. Blackwood, the estate's debts exceed
two million pounds. The sale is the only
path forward.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
I know what the numbers say, Ms. Voss.
I helped build those numbers.

Eleanor opens her briefcase on a dust-covered console table, pulling out a thick folder.

ELEANOR VOSS
The auction house arrives at noon. I need
you to identify any items with contested
ownership before then.

Thomas stares up at a large oil painting above the staircase — a portrait of a stern woman in Victorian dress.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
That painting. Harriet commissioned it
herself. It stays.

ELEANOR VOSS
Everything is on the table, Mr. Blackwood.
That was the agreement.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - LIBRARY - MORNING

Floor-to-ceiling bookshelves line every wall. Leather-bound volumes crowd the shelves. A mahogany desk dominates the center, papers scattered across its surface.

NADIA COLE (28, dark skin, intelligent brown eyes, natural hair, wearing jeans and a vintage band t-shirt under an open flannel) photographs the room with a professional camera. She moves with quiet reverence among the books.

NADIA COLE
(to herself)
First editions. Brontë, Dickens,
Wilkie Collins. She had taste.

She lifts a book carefully, opens the cover. A folded letter falls out. Nadia catches it, unfolds it slowly. Her expression shifts from curiosity to shock.

NADIA COLE
(reading quietly)
"My dearest Thomas, the house keeps
our secrets better than we ever could..."

She looks toward the door, folds the letter quickly, and slips it into her back pocket.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - DRAWING ROOM - MORNING

Furniture draped in white sheets creates ghostly shapes in dim light. Eleanor moves through the room with a clipboard, tagging items with numbered stickers.

RAYMOND CROSS (45, stocky build, thinning dark hair, sharp suspicious eyes, wearing an expensive overcoat over a silk shirt) watches from the doorway, arms folded.

RAYMOND CROSS
The Steinway alone is worth sixty
thousand. You do know that.

ELEANOR VOSS
I'm aware of every valuation in
this estate, Mr. Cross.

RAYMOND CROSS
Then you know my client has first
right of refusal on the piano
and the silver collection.

ELEANOR VOSS
Your client has a letter of intent.
That is not the same thing.

Raymond steps closer, lowering his voice.

RAYMOND CROSS
My client also has information about
how Harriet Hargrove actually died.
I suggest that buys him more than
a letter of intent.

Eleanor stops tagging. She turns to face him directly.

ELEANOR VOSS
Is that a threat, Mr. Cross?

RAYMOND CROSS
It's a negotiation.


EXT. HARGROVE ESTATE - GARDEN - MORNING

An overgrown Victorian garden. Dead roses cling to rusted iron trellises. A stone fountain, dry and cracked, stands at the center. Beyond, rolling countryside stretches to the horizon under heavy grey clouds.

Thomas sits on a weathered bench, staring at the fountain. He holds a small velvet box in his hands, turning it over and over.

ELEANOR VOSS (O.S.)
Mr. Blackwood? We need to discuss
the jewelry collection.

Thomas closes the box quickly, slips it into his jacket pocket.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
Coming.

He stands slowly, looks back at the fountain one last time. For a moment, something like guilt crosses his face.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - MASTER BEDROOM - DAY

A grand bedroom with heavy velvet curtains drawn. An ornate four-poster bed with silk coverings. Framed photographs line the vanity — all featuring the same stern woman from the portrait downstairs.

Nadia enters cautiously. She photographs the room, then notices a photograph that's been placed face-down on the nightstand. She turns it over.

The photograph shows Thomas Blackwood and Harriet Hargrove together, decades younger, standing before this very house. They look happy.

Nadia takes a photo of the photograph, then carefully replaces it face-down.

She opens the vanity drawer. Inside: a leather-bound journal, monogrammed H.H.

NADIA COLE
(whispering)
Hello, Harriet.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - KITCHEN - DAY

A vast Victorian kitchen with copper pots hanging from ceiling racks. A large farmhouse table sits in the center, now serving as Eleanor's makeshift command post, covered in documents and laptop.

Eleanor is on her phone, pacing.

ELEANOR VOSS
(into phone)
No, the probate court was clear.
Contested items remain in escrow
until — yes, I understand, but —
tell the auction house we proceed
at noon regardless.

She hangs up. Rubs her temples. Through the kitchen window, she can see Thomas in the garden.

Nadia enters through a service door, camera around her neck.

NADIA COLE
The upstairs inventory is done. Four
bedrooms, two bathrooms, the study.
Everything photographed and catalogued.

ELEANOR VOSS
Good. Any surprises?

NADIA COLE
(hesitating)
Define surprises.

Eleanor looks up sharply.

ELEANOR VOSS
Nadia. What did you find?

NADIA COLE
Harriet Hargrove kept a journal. And
I think it contradicts the official
cause of death.

Eleanor sets down her pen.

ELEANOR VOSS
Show me.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - MASTER BEDROOM - DAY

Eleanor and Nadia stand at the vanity. The journal is open between them. Eleanor reads, her expression hardening.

ELEANOR VOSS
"Heart palpitations growing worse.
Thomas insists on Dr. Marsh, but
I don't trust Marsh. He answers to
Raymond, not to me."

NADIA COLE
There are entries going back six
months before she died. She was
afraid, Eleanor. She wrote it down
because she didn't trust anyone
around her.

ELEANOR VOSS
This changes the legal standing of
the entire estate.

NADIA COLE
It changes a lot more than that.

A floorboard creaks in the hallway. Both women freeze. Eleanor moves quickly to the door, opens it.

The hallway is empty. But a shadow passes at the far end, near the stairs.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - GRAND STAIRCASE - DAY

Eleanor descends the staircase with purpose. Below, in the foyer, Raymond Cross is examining the oil painting of Harriet, reaching up to touch the frame.

ELEANOR VOSS
Don't touch that.

RAYMOND CROSS
(turning, smiling)
Just admiring the craftsmanship.
Remarkable condition for its age.

ELEANOR VOSS
The auction house handles all
physical inspection. You know
the protocol.

RAYMOND CROSS
Of course. I was merely passing
through.

He adjusts his overcoat and moves toward the drawing room. Eleanor watches him go, then looks up at the painting.

Harriet Hargrove stares back from the canvas with knowing, accusatory eyes.


EXT. HARGROVE ESTATE - FRONT DRIVE - DAY

A delivery van marked "ASHFORD AUCTION HOUSE" pulls up the gravel drive. Two workers begin unloading equipment — display stands, numbered paddles, a portable lectern.

Thomas watches from the portico, gripping the railing.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
(quietly, to himself)
Forgive me, Harriet.

Nadia appears beside him, camera in hand.

NADIA COLE
Mr. Blackwood? I found something
of yours in the library. A letter.

Thomas goes very still.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
What letter?

NADIA COLE
A letter from Harriet. Hidden in
a copy of Jane Eyre.

Thomas turns to her. His composure breaks — just for a moment.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
Did you read it?

NADIA COLE
Some of it. Enough to know that
you and Harriet were more than
business partners.

Thomas looks away, toward the garden.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
Everyone in this house has secrets,
Ms. Cole. The question is which
ones matter now that she's gone.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - DRAWING ROOM - DAY

The room is being transformed. Auction workers arrange chairs in rows. Display cases are set up along the walls. Items are being unwrapped from their dust sheets — silver candelabras, porcelain, crystal.

Eleanor enters and finds Raymond directing the workers.

ELEANOR VOSS
What are you doing? You don't have
authority to direct the setup.

RAYMOND CROSS
Someone had to. You were upstairs
reading bedtime stories.

Eleanor's jaw tightens.

ELEANOR VOSS
The journal is now evidence, Mr. Cross.
This estate sale may need to be
postponed pending a new inquest.

Raymond's smile vanishes.

RAYMOND CROSS
You can't do that. My client has
invested months in this acquisition.

ELEANOR VOSS
Your client's name appears seventeen
times in Harriet's journal. None of
them favorably. I suggest he find
a very good solicitor.

RAYMOND CROSS
(stepping closer, threatening)
You're making a mistake, Ms. Voss.
A very expensive mistake.

ELEANOR VOSS
I've been threatened by better men
than you represent, Mr. Cross.
The sale is suspended.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - LIBRARY - LATE AFTERNOON

Golden late-afternoon light pours through tall windows. Dust motes float in the beams. Nadia sits at the mahogany desk, Harriet's journal open before her, her own notebook beside it, writing furiously.

Thomas enters quietly. He stands in the doorway, watching her.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
She would have liked you.

Nadia looks up.

NADIA COLE
Harriet?

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
She always said the house needed
young people. People who could see
it for what it was, not just what
it was worth.

He crosses to the window, looks out at the garden.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
I loved her, you know. For thirty
years. She was the most brilliant,
most terrifying woman I ever knew.

NADIA COLE
Then why didn't you protect her?

Thomas turns. His eyes are wet.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
Because I was a coward. And by the
time I found my courage, Raymond's
people had already found Dr. Marsh.

NADIA COLE
Thomas. Are you saying what I think
you're saying?

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
I'm saying that journal is the most
important thing in this house. More
than the paintings, the silver, the
books. It's her voice. And it tells
the truth about what happened to her.

He reaches into his pocket and produces the velvet box from earlier. Opens it. Inside: an antique emerald ring.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
She left this for the person who
would finally listen. I think that
person is you.


INT. HARGROVE ESTATE - GRAND FOYER - EVENING

Fading twilight through the stained glass. Eleanor stands at the console table, phone to her ear. Her briefcase is packed. Auction equipment sits abandoned in the drawing room beyond.

ELEANOR VOSS
(into phone)
The coroner's office will need the
journal by morning. I'm having it
couriered tonight with full chain
of custody documentation.
(pause)
Yes. I'm recommending a full
criminal investigation.

She hangs up. Nadia descends the staircase, journal carefully wrapped in acid-free tissue paper, held in both hands.

NADIA COLE
It's ready. Every page photographed,
every entry transcribed.

ELEANOR VOSS
Good work, Nadia. This could take
months to resolve.

NADIA COLE
And the house? What happens to it?

ELEANOR VOSS
The estate sale is suspended
indefinitely. The house becomes
a crime scene.

They both look around the foyer — the dust sheets, the dark chandelier, the stern portrait watching from above.

NADIA COLE
She's still here, isn't she?
In every room.

ELEANOR VOSS
That's what estates are, Nadia.
They're just the shape that's left
when a person goes.

The front door opens. Thomas stands in the doorway, silhouetted against the evening sky.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
The auction workers have gone. Raymond
left an hour ago. It's just us and
the house now.

ELEANOR VOSS
Mr. Blackwood, I should inform you
that the contents of the journal may
implicate people close to you.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
I know. That's why I stayed.

He steps inside. The door closes behind him.

THOMAS BLACKWOOD
Whatever comes next, this house
deserves the truth. Harriet
deserves the truth.

ELEANOR VOSS
Then let's make sure she gets it.

The three of them stand in the foyer as darkness gathers. Above them, the portrait of Harriet Hargrove seems almost to smile.


EXT. HARGROVE ESTATE - WIDE SHOT - NIGHT

The estate from the outside. A single light burns in the library window. The rest of the house is dark, a massive Victorian silhouette against a moonlit sky. An owl calls from somewhere in the overgrown garden.

TITLE CARD: "VICTORIAN SHADOWS will continue..."


FADE OUT.


THE END
