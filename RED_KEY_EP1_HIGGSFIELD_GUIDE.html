● CINEMATIC STYLE — Extracted from Reference / Applied to All Shots
Artistic Medium
Photorealistic cinematic movie still — not illustrated, not stylized. Looks like it was captured by a real cinema camera.
Lighting Philosophy
Low-key, high contrast — deep black shadows, minimal fill, light sources earn every inch they illuminate. No accidental light.
Color Grade
Cool and desaturated base — teal-green in shadows, deep black crush, warm sources (fire/lantern) are isolated pops. Gritty, not pretty.
Camera / Lens
Digital cinema 2010s–2020s. Moderate depth of field — subject sharp, background softens. Physical lens effects: water on glass, slight distortion.
Film Grain
Visible digital noise in shadow areas — contributes to gritty tension. Not clean, not filtered. The grain is part of the story.
Physics / Texture
Real-world material texture visible — wet fabric weight, mud surface reflectivity, water splash physics, breathing in cold air. Physical presence.
Mood Signature
Tense, urgent, foreboding — every frame implies something worse is coming. The world is not safe. The light is not reassuring.
Higgsfield Style Tag
Use: cinematic, photorealistic, high contrast, desaturated, film grain, low-key lighting, anamorphic
